HOW POLICE STRENGTH AFFECTS NUMBER OF CRIMES

To Analyse if police strength has a role in determining the number of crimes against women in a state, we collected data of factors that could have a role in the following. The factors selected are :-

■ Police Strength
■ Total crimes against women



Fig1:Scatter Plot (X axis->Police Strength, Y axis-> Total crimes against women)

A correlation of .96117 is observed between total crimes and police strength. The data set used is from 2001-2014. It is seen through the scatter plot that total crimes have a highly positive correlation with the police strength indicating that the crimes increases as police strength increases which is a difficult statement to believe.

Demonstrating an effect of police numbers on crime is an issue beset with problems of causality, because there are many issues that might affect both police numbers and the crime, such as economic cycles or social change. Furthermore, it is entirely possible - indeed likely - that more crime actually causes more police, in the sense that all else equal, an increase in the rate of crime will lead to more officers being hired. In such cases it may look as if higher numbers of police cause more crime, and it is important to examine temporal sequences.

Another Understanding that could be drawn out is that higher number of police can be an indicator of high crimes reported because there is a huge gap in the number of crime incidence and number of crimes reported.More police strength can lead to people report more crimes.

It is expected that a negative effect of police strength on the number of crimes/crime rate could be observed at very local level in a particular region.
