KIDNAPPING AND ABDUCTION

Kidnapping and abduction is the third most common crime against women in India. The number of kidnappings and abductions of women and girls have jumped a whopping 163.8% since 2002. Abductions of women for marriage accounted for about 40% of all cases of kidnappings and abductions in India, according to recently released government data.


The following graph shows the states with maximum number of kidnapping and abduction cases reported. As per the data by National Bureau of crime Assam,Delhi,Uttar Pradesh,Bihar,West Bengal,Rajasthan can be considered a threatful place for women but the higher figures could be because of the large population in some states like Uttar pradesh,Bihar,Rajasthan and West Bengal.But it's shocking that the small states/UTs like Delhi and Assam also have huge number of crimes reported.


No of kidnapping and abduction per 100000 Women indicate that Jammu & Kashmir have a similar number of cases from 2011-2015 while Northern state like Delhi and Chandigarh having a high increase in the cases registered from 2011 - 2014 with a sudden fall in 2015.Figures in Delhi are the maximum from 2011-2014 with a major difference from the second highest Chandigarh.Assam has seen slow rises in the no of kidnapping and abduction per 100000 women and tops in 2015 as all the other states shows a fall from 2014 to 2015 except Assam.


Comparing the total number of cases and number of cases per 100000 women bring to us new states like Haryana, Changigarh and Jammu and kashmir.This graph let's us see how state with less number of cases like Chandigarh and Jammu & kashmir can be dangerous as the population plays a greater role in the determination of hotspots. It is observed that Uttar Pradesh,Assam,Haryana and Bihar has significant figure in both the list making it a major hotspot for kidnapping and abduction cases in India. Delhi and Jaamu & Kashmir,Chandigarh have very less relative value in no of cases with other states but are a hotspot of crime when female population is taken into consideration.
