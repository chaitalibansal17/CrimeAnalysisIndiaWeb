DOWRY DEATH

Dowry deaths are deaths of women who are murdered or driven to suicide by continuous harassment and torture by husbands and in-laws in an effort to extort an increased dowry.Most of these suicides are by hanging, poisoning or by fire. Sometimes the woman is killed by setting herself on fireand sometimes disguised as suicide or accident. Death by burning of Indian women have been more frequently attributed to dowry conflicts.In dowry deaths, the groomâ€™s family is the perpetrator of murder or suicide.

India has by far the highest number of dowry related deaths in the world according to Indian National Crime Record Bureau. In 2012, 8,233 dowry death cases were reported across India.This means a bride was burned every 90 minutes, or dowry issues cause 1.4 deaths per year per 100,000 women in India.




The following graph shows the states with maximum number of dowry death cases reported. As per the data by National Bureau of crime, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh,Bihar are the states with major dowry deaths reported.


No of dowry deaths per 100000 Women shows Bihar top in the list,following closely is Uttar pradesh which crosses Bihar in 2015 to top. The number of cases per female population has seen similar values with slight differences for all the states in all the 5 years.Even after a lot of Education and awareness programs the rate of dowry death is increasing in states like Bihar and Uttar pradesh.


Comparing the total number of cases and number of cases per 100000 women brings state like Jharkhand, Haryana and odisha into the picture.This graph let's us see how state with less number of cases can be dangerous as the population plays a greater role in the determination of hotspots.For example: Haryana has 3 times lesser no of cases reported as compared to Madhya pradesh but it has higher rate than Madhya pradesh. It is observed that Uttar Pradesh and Bihar have the highest rate making them the biggest hotspot for dowry deaths in India.Reason could be maximum of rural population.
